Who I Am

I am an IT professional with over 10 years of experience supporting end-user computing environments, troubleshooting hardware and software issues, deploying operating systems, managing peripherals, and maintaining business IT systems. My current focus is on building practical expertise in Identity & Access Management, Microsoft cloud technologies, endpoint management, and automation through hands-on engineering projects and continuous professional development.

Rather than deploying technologies using default wizards, I build out complex hybrid and cloud-only tenant environments designed to systematically test governance boundary limits, endpoint compliance enforcement rules, and automated identity lifecycles.

The Intersection of Security & Infrastructure

My approach to systems administration is heavily shaped by my advanced academic background in cybersecurity. Having studied threat vectors, perimeter vulnerabilities, and compliance frameworks at a postgraduate level, I looked at modern cloud systems and realized that Identity is the new security perimeter.

This realization is what drives my continuous learning path. Whether I am analyzing Linux system mechanics, parsing SIEM dashboards, or studying Microsoft cloud architecture, my end goal is always the same: understanding how to minimize a corporation's digital attack surface. I don't just want to manage endpoints and users; I want to govern them securely.

My Engineering & Lab Methodology

"This portfolio focuses on solving business problems through practical implementation, validation testing, and evidence-driven documentation. Each project is structured around real-world scenarios designed to mirror enterprise environments. Every configuration is built with a strict Zero Trust mindset—enforcing least-privilege administrative access controls, isolating application data boundaries, executing programmatic automation over manual tasks, and logging verbose diagnostic event codes to trace system drift."
